The 3 silent mechanisms behind memory decline

Researchers have identified what's really happening inside your brain after 50.

01

Neuroinflammation

After 50, microglia — the brain's immune cells — become overactive. This chronic low-grade inflammation damages neural connections and slows communication between brain cells.

02

Reduced cerebral blood flow

Less blood to the brain means less oxygen and fewer nutrients reaching the neurons that power your memory and concentration.

03

Oxidative stress

Free radicals accumulate faster than your body can neutralize them. Brain cells are especially vulnerable — they consume 20% of your oxygen but have limited antioxidant defenses.
